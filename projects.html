Project Overview

A comprehensive analysis of employee attrition, satisfaction, and performance using IBM's HR Analytics dataset. This project aims to help decision-makers proactively address workforce management challenges through data-driven insights.

Key Objectives

  • Evaluate Employee Performance
  • Analyze Employee Attrition
  • Assess Monthly Income
  • Measure Job Satisfaction

Dataset Information

  • Source: Kaggle IBM HR Analytics Attrition Dataset
  • Size: 1,470 employee records
  • Features: 35 columns
  • Format: CSV file

Methodology & Results

Model Accuracy:

  • Attrition Prediction: 96% accuracy
  • Salary Prediction: 89% R² score
  • Performance Rating: 94% accuracy
  • Work-life Balance: 92% accuracy

Dashboard

Dashboard on Tableau Public

Key Findings

  • Employees closer to the workplace showed higher attrition rates
  • Fresh employees were more prone to attrition compared to experienced staff
  • Lower monthly income correlated with higher attrition rates
  • Overtime work significantly impacted attrition rates

Transportation Efficiency Study | Python & Power BI

Transportation efficiency study using CO2 emissions and car registeration data across Europe to find out how can we reduce polution caused by cars.



Transportation Efficiency Study

Data Collection

To gather the necessary data for this study, I utilized ChatGPT, which provided me with a variety of data sources. After reviewing these, I decided to focus my analysis on car registration data collected across Europe in 2023. The dataset I selected can be found at the following link:
Link to the Dataset.

Data Preprocessing and Cleaning with Python

After downloading the dataset, I loaded it into a pandas DataFrame to begin the preprocessing and cleaning phase. The raw data contained many missing values, especially in important fields like car make and model, which are crucial for the analysis. Additionally, there were several unnecessary columns that did not contribute to the study, so I removed them to streamline the dataset. I handled the missing values by dropping records where essential fields were empty. Once the dataset was cleaned, I exported it to a CSV file for further use in building the dashboard.

Dashboard

Insights and Summary

  1. Cars Registered & CO2 Emissions:
    • 10.73M cars are registered in Europe, with an average CO2 emission of 106.67 g/km and fuel consumption of 5.41 g/km.
    • Germany, Italy, and France lead in both registrations and CO2 emissions.
  2. Fuel Type & Electric Vehicles:
    • Petrol cars dominate at 56.03%, followed by diesel (16.98%). Electric cars account for 15.55%, highlighting the need for further growth to reduce emissions.
  3. CO2 Emissions by Engine Size & Fuel Consumption:
    • Larger engines produce more CO2, and higher fuel consumption correlates with higher emissions. Reducing fuel-hungry vehicles is critical.
  4. Supercars vs. Regular Cars:
    • Supercars like the Aston Martin Valkyrie and Bugatti Chiron have very high CO2 emissions, averaging over 400 g/km, but they are rare (Valkyrie: 11, Chiron: 1).
    • In contrast, everyday cars like the Sandero (223,289 registered) and TROC (177,749 registered) have a much larger presence and contribute heavily to CO2 emissions despite having lower per-car emissions.
  5. Electric Conversion Recommendation:
    • High-volume, high-emission cars like the Sandero and TROC need to transition to electric models, which produce zero emissions, to reduce the overall environmental impact of transportation.

Covid-19
Overview | Power BI

This COVID-19 dashboard visually summarizes global pandemic data, including total cases, recoveries, and deaths. Despite initial data challenges, extensive cleaning with Power Query and advanced DAX functions enabled the creation of insightful and dynamic visualizations.



Covid-19 Overview

This COVID-19 Dashboard was created after overcoming significant challenges due to incomplete data sets that contained many missing values. To prepare this comprehensive visual representation of pandemic statistics required extensive data cleaning using Power Query—a powerful tool within Excel that enables complex transformations and data shaping workflows.

Following the initial cleaning process using Power Query's capabilities to filter out irrelevant information or correct inconsistencies within the dataset was crucial.

After ensuring that the dataset was clean and usable, Data Analysis Expressions (DAX) functions were employed to create new measures that could provide deeper insights into COVID-19's impact across different regions over time. DAX functions are advanced formulas used within Power BI environments that allow for dynamic aggregations and calculations based on user interactions with reports or dashboards.

These measures were essential in developing meaningful visuals such as trend lines showing total cases over time or recovery rates which are critical in understanding both historical patterns as well as current situations regarding COVID-19 spread globally.

Summary

In summary, this dashboard provides an at-a-glance overview of global COVID-19 statistics through various data visualization methods. It allows viewers to quickly grasp trends over time, compare regional impacts, and understand key figures related to testing, recovery rates, and fatalities.

This business dashboard offers a concise overview of sales performance with visualizations like maps, pie charts, bar graphs, and line graphs. Key metrics such as total sales, profits, and units sold are prominently displayed. It enables quick assessment and data-driven decision-making.



Sales Analysis Dashboard using Excel

Main Highlights

  1. Map of Sales per State: Highlights regional sales performance, helping identify top-performing areas.
  2. Pie Charts for Sales by Segment and Category: Breaks down sales distribution, showing which segments and categories contribute most to revenue.

To create this dashboard, I used several powerful tools:

  • Excel Charts were used to visually represent the data, making it easy to understand and interpret key metrics and trends.
