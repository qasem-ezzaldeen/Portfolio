Digital Marketing Campaign Overview

This Tableau dashboard provides an in-depth analysis of a digital marketing campaign's performance for February 2021. The dashboard includes key metrics such as revenue, marketing spend, Return on Marketing Investment (ROMI), Click-Through Rate (CTR), and Conversion Rate (CR).

Main Highlights

  • Top Metrics: The top row of the dashboard displays the overall campaign's key performance indicators:
    • Revenue: $42.89M
    • Marketing Spend: $30.59M
    • ROMI: 0.40
    • CTR: 0.19%
    • CR: 0.27%
    • CPC: $10.20
    • CAC: $466.47

Detail by Source

Visuals in the "Detail by Source" section include a breakdown of revenue by marketing sources such as Facebook, Google, Instagram, Media Banners, and YouTube. The pie chart highlights the revenue contribution from each source, with specific dollar amounts labeled.

Detail by Channel

This section categorizes the marketing efforts into channels like Influencer, Media, Search, and Social. The visuals provide insights into which channels are most effective in terms of revenue and ROMI.

Click-Through Rate (CTR) and Conversion Rate (CR)

The bottom section includes line charts that track the daily CTR and CR throughout February. This data visualization allows for trend analysis across different sources and channels, helping identify which marketing strategies are performing best over time.

Key Insights

  • Revenue Distribution: The pie charts show how revenue is distributed among sources and channels, offering a clear picture of which areas are generating the most income.
  • ROMI Comparison: The bar charts illustrate the ROMI for different sources and channels, helping identify the most cost-effective marketing efforts.
  • Daily Performance Trends: The line charts for CTR and CR track performance fluctuations, providing actionable insights into daily campaign effectiveness.

This dashboard allows for a comprehensive view of the digital marketing campaign's performance, helping guide future strategies for maximizing ROI.

Financial Report Dashboard

Main Highlights

  • Key Metrics: The top section displays important financial metrics:
    • Sales: $118,726,350
    • Expenses: $101,832,648
    • Profit: $16,893,702

Regional Performance

The map visualizes sales performance across different regions. For example, Canada and the United States show their respective sales figures, giving a geographical perspective on market performance.

Profit Analysis

  • Total Profit: The line chart tracks profit trends over different quarters, providing a clear view of how profit has changed over time.
  • Profit Margin per Product: The bar chart presents the profit margins for various products, highlighting which products contributed most to profitability.

Revenue vs. Expenses

The line chart in this section illustrates the revenue and expenses trends over time, allowing for a comparative analysis to understand periods of high profitability and increased spending.

Expenses by Segment

Filters

The left-side panel includes various filters for exploring the data:

Covid-19 Overview

This COVID-19 Dashboard was created after overcoming significant challenges due to incomplete data sets that contained many missing values. To prepare this comprehensive visual representation of pandemic statistics required extensive data cleaning using Power Queryâ€”a powerful tool within Excel that enables complex transformations and data shaping workflows.

Following the initial cleaning process using Power Query's capabilities to filter out irrelevant information or correct inconsistencies within the dataset was crucial.

After ensuring that the dataset was clean and usable, Data Analysis Expressions (DAX) functions were employed to create new measures that could provide deeper insights into COVID-19's impact across different regions over time. DAX functions are advanced formulas used within Power BI environments that allow for dynamic aggregations and calculations based on user interactions with reports or dashboards.

These measures were essential in developing meaningful visuals such as trend lines showing total cases over time or recovery rates which are critical in understanding both historical patterns as well as current situations regarding COVID-19 spread globally.

Summary

In summary, this dashboard provides an at-a-glance overview of global COVID-19 statistics through various data visualization methods. It allows viewers to quickly grasp trends over time, compare regional impacts, and understand key figures related to testing, recovery rates, and fatalities.

Sales Analysis | Excel

This business dashboard offers a concise overview of sales performance with visualizations like maps, pie charts, bar graphs, and line graphs. Key metrics such as total sales, profits, and units sold are prominently displayed. It enables quick assessment and data-driven decision-making.



Sales Analysis Dashboard using Excel

Main Highlights

  1. Map of Sales per State: Highlights regional sales performance, helping identify top-performing areas.
  2. Pie Charts for Sales by Segment and Category: Breaks down sales distribution, showing which segments and categories contribute most to revenue.
  3. Bar Graphs for Top Products and Regional Sales: Displays top-selling products and sales by region, aiding in product and market strategy.
  4. Line Graph for Profits Over Time: Tracks profit trends, providing insights into financial performance and growth over time.
  5. Slicers: Allow users to filter data dynamically by various dimensions such as time, region, or product category, enhancing interactivity and focused analysis.
  6. KPIs (Key Performance Indicators): Display critical metrics like total sales, profits, and units sold at a glance, enabling quick assessment of business performance.

Tools Used

To create this dashboard, I used several powerful tools:

  • Power Query was utilized to explore and clean the data, ensuring it was accurate and ready for analysis.
  • Pivot Tables were employed to summarize and analyze the data, allowing for detailed insights into sales performance across different segments and categories.
  • Excel Charts were used to visually represent the data, making it easy to understand and interpret key metrics and trends.
